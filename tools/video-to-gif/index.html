Frequently Asked Questions

What video formats are supported? ▼

This tool supports MP4 (H.264), WebM, MOV, AVI, and any video format your browser can natively play. For best compatibility, use MP4 videos which work reliably across all modern browsers.

Why is my GIF file so large? ▼

GIF is an old format that stores each frame individually without modern video compression. To shrink your GIF: reduce FPS to 8–10, lower the output width, trim to 2–3 seconds, or set quality to 50–60%.

Can I convert longer videos? ▼

The tool caps at 30-second clips to keep file sizes manageable. For longer videos, consider using a proper video-to-GIF desktop tool. The browser-based approach works best for short, shareable clips.

Is my video uploaded anywhere? ▼

No. Everything happens in your browser. The video is loaded as a local blob URL, frames are extracted via Canvas, and the GIF is encoded by the gifshot library — all client-side. Your video never leaves your computer.
